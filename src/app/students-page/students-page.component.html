decision criteria

We want to help as many organizations as possible. Unfortunately, we do not have the capacity to accept every project due to our limited resources. These are the primary criteria we use to determine project fit:

1. Impact

How much will the project impact the community? We prefer projects that will provide the maximal benefit to the target community.

2. Necessity

How vital is the project to the non-profit organization, and does the organization have other means to complete the project? We want to ensure that we are helping organizations that truly need our services.

3. Scope

Is completing the project feasible within our timeline? We want to ensure that we can finish the project and deliver quality results by then end of our development cycle.

4. Technical Fit

Is the project technically challenging and fit well with our team's areas of expertise?

5. Credibility

Is it clear that the project will have an impact? We like to see evidence that supports this!

process

1

Step 1

Submit your application

Tell us a little bit about yourself and why you're interested in joining Blueprint.

2

Step 2

We view your application

We make sure to go through every application that we receive for joining the Blueprint team. Once we have made a decision, we will send you an email with details about next steps.

3

Step 3

Coffee chat with one of us!

Let's grab coffee! Here, we will take some time to learn more about you and do some quick assessments of your technical abilities. You can also take this time to ask us any questions you might have!

4

Step 4

Online interview (Developers only)

The last step of the process is to assess your technical abilities at a deeper level. We will ask you questions that are specific to topics that you feel most strongly in or have the most prior experience in.

5

Step 5

Offer!

Congrats! You made it. Within a few days of your online interview, we will contact you by email to send you an offer. Here, we will outline the details of your project, ask you for your username preference, and request some sizes from you for some Blueprint swag!

faq

What can I expect by being a part of Blueprint?

At the very least, you can expect to make new friends, to be constantly challenged with new technical problems, and be a part of a tight-knit team that is making a difference.

I don't have a lot of experience in software development. Should I still apply?

Yes! Blueprint is a great way to gain more experience as a software developer. You will be surrounded by peers that are willing to help you at every step of the way. As a team, we care deeply about your ability to learn and your passion for social good -- less so about past experience.
